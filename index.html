Fuel & Range Calculator Rule of Thirds
Fuel Efficiency Tips
Rule of Thirds: Use 1/3 going, 1/3 returning, 1/3 reserve. Never dip below your reserve.
Speed vs. Burn: Most planing hulls burn dramatically more above hull speed. Reduce RPM 10% → often saves 20–30% fuel.
Factor In: Current, headwinds, load weight, sea state, AC/generator load — all increase real-world burn.

Mayday Procedure MEMORIZE THIS
Transmit on VHF Channel 16 at full power (25W):
"MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY"
"This is [VESSEL NAME] [VESSEL NAME] [VESSEL NAME]"
"MAYDAY [VESSEL NAME]"
"My position is [LAT/LON or BEARING/DISTANCE FROM LANDMARK]"
"I am [NATURE OF DISTRESS]"
"I have [NUMBER] persons on board"
"I require immediate assistance"
"Over"
Wait 1 minute, repeat if no response. DSC-equipped radios: press DISTRESS button for 5 seconds.

Fire Protocol
1. Shut off fuel + ventilate
2. Direct extinguisher at base
3. Aim for escape route behind you
4. Don PFDs if abandoning ship
5. Mayday if not controlled
Man Overboard
1. Shout "MAN OVERBOARD"
2. Press MOB button on GPS
3. Throw ring/buoy immediately
4. Keep eyes on victim
5. Call Mayday on Ch 16
6. Williamson turn or figure 8
Taking On Water
1. Locate source, reduce speed
2. Pump bilge (manual backup)
3. Plug with rags/damage control
4. Don PFDs
5. Mayday if uncontrolled
6. Prepare to abandon ship
Engine Failure
1. Deploy anchor if near hazards
2. Check fuel, kill switch, circuit
3. Check raw water impeller
4. Alert nearby vessels via VHF
5. Call Sea Tow or BoatUS
6. Mayday if in danger
DSC / EPIRB Quick Reference
DSC Radio: Hold DISTRESS button 5 seconds → automatically transmits your MMSI + GPS position to all DSC-equipped vessels and Coast Guard.
EPIRB (406 MHz): Activate manually or automatic on water contact. Registered MMSI links to your vessel. Signal detected by COSPAS-SARSAT satellites.
PLB: Personal Locator Beacon — clip to PFD. Registered to individual. Pull antenna, press button.
